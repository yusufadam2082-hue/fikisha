Order to payout algorithm (simple terms)

1) Customer places order
The app saves the order, items, delivery fee, and payment details.

2) Payment is confirmed
If payment succeeds (or COD is accepted), the order becomes active for fulfillment.

3) Store prepares order
The store accepts the order, prepares items, and marks it ready.

4) Driver is assigned and delivers
A driver is assigned, picks up the order, and updates delivery status until completed.

5) Order is marked DELIVERED
This is the key trigger. When delivery is completed, the app runs accounting calculations.

6) Accounting snapshot is created
The app calculates and saves:
- Customer total
- Merchant platform fee
- Merchant net payout
- Driver payout
- Platform revenue and delivery margin

7) Payout eligibility is checked
Only orders that are delivered and not refunded are payout-eligible.

8) Payout entries are created
If eligible:
- Merchant payout entry is created as PENDING
- Driver payout entry is created as PENDING (if a driver exists)
- Platform revenue entry is recorded

9) Admin payout center groups balances
The system groups pending entries by merchant and driver so admin sees who is owed and how much.

10) Admin creates payout batch
Admin selects eligible pending entries and creates a payout batch (merchant or driver).

11) Admin marks batch as paid
Before finalizing, the app re-checks entries are still valid (not refunded/cancelled).
Then statuses move from PENDING to PAID and payment timestamps are saved.

12) Reconciliation updates
Dashboards update:
- Paid vs pending merchant payouts
- Paid vs pending driver payouts
- Total platform revenue
- Outstanding liabilities

One-line summary:
Order is placed, delivered, converted into accounting records, then paid out through controlled admin batches with status tracking and safety checks.
