Fikisha Business Model

Platform Strategy, Revenue Design, and Scale Plan

Date: March 16, 2026

1. Business Thesis

Fikisha is a multi-sided local commerce platform that aggregates customer demand, routes demand to merchants, orchestrates last-mile delivery through drivers, and captures platform margin by controlling payment collection and merchant settlement.

Demand aggregation Merchant enablement Logistics orchestration Settlement-led monetization Admin-grade governance

2. Core Value Proposition

For Customers

  • Convenient multi-store ordering
  • Transparent delivery tracking
  • Reliable service and status updates

For Merchants

  • Additional digital sales channel
  • Managed order pipeline and delivery dispatch
  • Settlement visibility via admin accounting flow

For Platform

  • Commission-based scalable margin
  • Data advantage on demand and fulfillment
  • Cross-role control with auditable operations

4. Settlement Logic

Financial flow: Customer pays platform -> platform recognizes gross revenue -> platform deducts commission -> platform settles net payable to merchant by cycle.

Base formula per cycle:

  • Gross Merchant Sales = sum of non-cancelled order totals
  • Platform Commission = Gross Merchant Sales × Commission %
  • Merchant Net Payable = Gross Merchant Sales - Platform Commission
  • Pending Payout = Merchant Net Payable - Recorded Payouts

7. Go-to-Market Strategy

Phase 1: Local Density (0-6 months)

  • Concentrate in one city zone for reliability
  • Onboard anchor merchants by category
  • Run referral loops for first-order customers
  • Guarantee delivery windows to build trust

Phase 2: Network Expansion (6-18 months)

  • Expand to adjacent zones by demand clusters
  • Introduce merchant subscription tiers
  • Launch sponsored placement products
  • Add SLA-based driver incentive mechanics

8. Operating Model and Governance

  • Control Tower: Admin portal manages stores, drivers, logs, and accounting settlements.
  • Risk Gates: Role-based auth, log trails, status transitions, and payout ledger controls.
  • Settlement Discipline: Cycle-based payout review and mark-paid workflow.
  • Data Loop: Operational data informs pricing, driver allocation, and merchant performance management.

12. 90-Day Action Roadmap

  1. Stabilize settlement ops: monthly close checklist + merchant payout statement templates.
  2. Add accounting exports: CSV/PDF for payout and commission reports.
  3. Launch merchant performance dashboard: fulfillment speed, cancellation, rating trends.
  4. Introduce driver incentive policy tied to on-time and completion quality.
  5. Instrument KPI dashboard for executive weekly review (GMV, take rate, payout lag, repeat rate).

13. Final Recommendation

Fikisha’s strongest strategic edge is the integrated control of order flow, delivery execution, and merchant settlement. The near-term objective should be to maximize city-level density and service reliability while institutionalizing settlement trust. This positions the business for scalable unit economics and defensible marketplace network effects.
